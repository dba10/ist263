Mali is one of the largest countries in West Africa. Many people from countries like Ivory Coast, Senegal, Guinea, and Burkina Faso have origins that trace back to Mali. Assimi Goïta is the current president of Mali and has made significant contributions to the country. For instance, as mentioned before, our official language is now Bambara. Under Assimi Goïta’s leadership, Mali has removed French troops from the country, which marks a significant milestone for us. Some of our national dishes include “Tigadegi Nan,” “Tô,” and “Zame.” My personal favorite is my mother's Zame!

Malian Dishes.

Traditional music in Mali has a rich history and is ingrained in the customs of many different ethnic groups. Instruments like the ngoni (a string instrument), balafon (a xylophone), and the kora (a harp-lute) contribute to the distinctive tone of Malian music. My favorite Malian artists are Oumou Sangare and Sidiki Diabate. These two artists are well-known in West Africa.
